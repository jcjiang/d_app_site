So, uh, I guess if you're here already, I can tell you a bit about myself?

I'm a first year student here at Dartmouth College, located in the bustling metropolitan city of Hanover, NH. I come from Walnut, California, which straddles the boundaries between LA and the OC.

Currently (as that is always the keyword when it comes to Dartmouth and majors) I am majoring in Computer Science modified with Digital Arts, so I can take those Studio Art classes without receiving several Concerned Parent Lectures. With the amount of time I spend excitedly talking about, well, anything from cool new ways to tell stories and create narratives (video games! code! fan fiction!) to the popularity of cultural medias in an international context, I am also majoring in Comparative Literature.

I've taken CS52 Full-Stack Web Development, CS50 Software Engineering, and CS10 in my time at Dartmouth. I'm getting in more into design in general, so I spend a lot of time messing around with Illustrator and Figma/Sketch.

I'm more about making specific technologies that are in collaboration with other fields (literature, art, scientific research.) That's because I think programming and tech is most important when they are combined with other things, improving or solving long-existing problems in topics like sustainability, human interaction, etc.
